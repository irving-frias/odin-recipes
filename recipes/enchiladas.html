Enchiladas

Description

Enchiladas are a traditional Mexican dish consisting of corn tortillas rolled around a filling and covered with a savory sauce. The fillings can vary, including meats, cheese, beans, potatoes, vegetables, and seafood. The sauce can range from mild to spicy, typically made with chili peppers, tomatoes, and spices.

Ingredients

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups cooked shredded chicken
  • 1 cup grated cheese (Monterey Jack or cheddar)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 2 cups enchilada sauce
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. In a skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Lightly fry each tortilla until soft and pliable, then set aside.
  3. In a bowl, mix shredded chicken with cumin, salt, and pepper. Add chopped onions and half of the grated cheese.
  4. Pour 1/2 cup of enchilada sauce into the bottom of a baking dish.
  5. Place a portion of the chicken mixture onto each tortilla, roll it up, and place it seam-side down in the baking dish.
  6. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas and sprinkle with the remaining cheese.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the sauce is bubbling.
  8. Garnish with sour cream and chopped cilantro before serving.
